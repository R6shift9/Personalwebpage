Phillip Stephen Smith

Thank you for checking out my webpage! This webpage is designed to give you a brief summary of my educational background and work experience. I graduated from California State University San Marcos in 2013 where I Majored in Political Science and Minored in Music Technology.

After graduation, I went to go work as an Account Executive for a small logistics company that specialized in LTL to Canada. I developed my own book of business through a combination of inside and outside sales. Since the company was small, I had to handle all aspects of the business cycle. I would prospect and close accounts, set up the billing, provide rates, provide customer service and on occasion set up pick ups and deliveries. I would regularly visit my active accounts and check in on other accounts to try and develop more business. Radiant Global Logistics acquired our company in 2015, they reduced our operations staff and it became hard to maintain my accounts so I decided to try something new.

I went to work for a hardware/software provider in the payments industry as an account manager. The position was a considered an internal farm program for their outside representatives as they had a wide variety of products and services. While I was there I worked on the indirect sales channel by managing the relationships with the companies distributors. I managed two distributors and supported six others, which was 60% of the company's business. I created reports for my accounts, booked purchase orders, attended trade shows and monitored inventory. I also handled incoming emails and phone calls for general sales inquiries, provide support for current users, supported outside sales representatives clients and helped 3rd parties integrate our hardware into their payments solution. After my six month review, I didn't see any growth opportunities in the near or intermediate term, so I decided to leave.

I accepted an Account Executive position for one of the largest payments processors in North America. The role was 100% inside sales and required me to prospect and close businesses that had customers who paid with corporate purchasing cards. While I was there I learned how to read and interpret credit card statements, PCI rules surrounding accepting credit card payments and how to get merchants the best possible rates for accepting electronic payments. After working there for four months I decided to explore other options.

Since leaving I have been looking for better sales opportunities as well as Analyst and Web developer positions. I have been studying web design, which is how I built this webpage. You can check out the school section to see what courses I have completed. This website is coded using only HTML/CSS, feel free to click the logos to learn more about the companies I've worked for and educational background.
